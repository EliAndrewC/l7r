Shadowlands Taint
=================

Shadowlands taint damage is taken by exposure to things which are themselves tainted.  The mechanics for taking Shadowlands taint vary based on the situation, e.g. actually being in the Shadowlands, fighting tainted creatures, descending into Jigoku, etc.

The taint is measured in 0.1 increments.  Until you reach 1.0 taint, your body can fight it off and eventually resume being untainted.  Once you reach 1.0 taint then that is the lowest you can ever go again, and characters with 1.0 taint lose many of their school and professiona abilities.  Humans will die at 6.0 taint.

When removed from exposure to the Shadowlands taint, the human body will purge itself of 0.1 point of taint every 10 days.  That said, there are other factors which accelerate the purification proces.  When determining how long it actually takes to purify yourself of this next 0.1 amount, make a precepts roll.  The TN of this roll is 5 + 50 * (the highest your Shadowlands taint score has ever been), e.g. if the most Shadowlands taint you've ever accumulated is 0.4 points, then the TN of this precents roll is 5 + 50 * 0.4 for a total of 25.

Many things can modify this roll:
* Everything which normally gives bonuses to precepts (e.g. adding twice your honor) boosts this roll.
* Wearing a jade amulet blessed by a priest for the duration gives a free raise.
* Taking ritual purification baths in a shrine or temple multiple times per day gives two free raises.  (Missing some number of these baths might reduce this to only one free raise or elimninate the bonus entirely.)
* Monks and priests who have sworn religious vows get a free raise.  (Not a free raise per vow, and only those living a monastic lifestyle get this bonus - those who have sworn religious vows while e.g. continuing to serve as samurai do not.)

For every 5 by which you exceed the TN of the roll, reduce the time by 1 day (to a minimum of 1 day).  Make another roll after that many days has passed, as the cycle repeats.
