Spirit encounters
=================

When encountering supernatural entities so strange that they warp the nature of reality itself, these rules may be used in place of normal skill and combat rolls.

## Different rules for dice rolls

Spirit encounters don't use normal XkY rules, where you roll the skill plus ring and then keep the ring.  Here you roll more dice but keep fewer.  Every roll uses two rings: you always use Void and add one of the other 4 rings.  Every roll also uses two different skills - these skill may not correspond to their usual rings.

All normal bonuses still apply, including rolled dice, free raises, and other bonuses given by advantages and schools.  The only exceptions are bonuses from Honor and Recognition, which do not add to any of your skill rolls except where stated otherwise.  However, the only dice counted are ones which roll at least one 10.  All other dice are ignored.

For example, suppose Kitsuki Dairoku takes the "evaluate options" action.  (A more complete explanation of this action is below, but for now we'll just focus on the dice mechanics.)  That roll is a Water action, which in this example scenario uses investigation + underworld.  The character in question has the Discerning advantage and is a Kitsuki magistrate who gets +1k0 on investigation rolls.

Dairoku has 4 investigation, 3 underworld, 4 Water, and 4 Void.  Combined with the extra rolled die from the Kitsuki school, he rolls a total of 16 dice and then adds a flat bonus of +10 from the two free raises he has from the Discerning advantage.  Supposing that he rolls:

> 1, 1, 1, 2, 2, 4, 5, 5, 5, 6, 6, 7, 8, 9, 15, 19

The only dice which count are the 15 and the 19, so combined with his flat +10 bonus, Dairoku has rolled a total of 44.  He may spend free raises from his 3rd Dan Technique as usual, the same as with any other investigation roll.

Most bushi will receive a free raise to each roll if they are at least 3rd Dan.  Most bushi will then receive an extra free raise if they have achieved 5th Dan.  The GM will let a player know whether they are expected to apply their school techniques directly or use this bonus instead.  For example, a Kitsuki magistrate may use their 3rd Dan technique (as in the above example), but would receive a free raise to every roll for being 5th Dan since the technique wouldn't apply.  Conversely, a Mirumoto bushi would receive a free raise for being at least 3rd Dan, but may use their 5th Dan technique directly.


## Wound checks

PCs do not make wound checks anytime they take damage.  Instead they make a single wound check at the end of each round if they have any light wounds, regardless of whether they've taken damage this round.

In most spirit encounters, wound checks are made as honor checks, i.e. rolling dice equal to twice your honor, keeping Honor.  All normal bonuses to wound checks, such as free raises and extra rolled dice, may be applied to these checks.

For example, Ikoma Kenatsu has 5 honor, giving him a base 10k5 honor check.  He gets an extra rolled die from his 1st Dan technique, and may spend free raises from this 3rd Dan technique after he sees the result of the roll.

Serious wounds are not dealt against your normal pool of health.  Instead, when taking 1 or more serious wounds, each wound is dealt randomly to one of the last skills you used prior to taking the serious wound.  Such wounds permanently alter your character's ability to perceive reality in the same way as anyone else.  The GM can provide examples of what kinds of things happen to PCs who take serious wounds in this way.

There are other forms of damage that can be taken in spiritual encounters when PCs take serious wounds (e.g. connection damage), but unless stated otherwise, players should assume that what they are facing is skill damage.


## Scenes, rounds, and elemental paths

Spirit encounters are divided into "scenes".  Much like scenes from a dream, the PCs will usually understand where they are and have some sense of what's happening around them, even if they don't remember how they specifically arrived in their current location or how they could get back to it later.  Scenes are divided into rounds - sometimes a scene will last until an objective is completed, an other times a scene will last for a predefined number of rounds.

Every character takes a single action each round.  In the first round of a scene, each PC chooses a single physical ring: Water, Fire, Earth, or Air.  The round progresses through those rings in that order.  Spirit encounters are turbulent, and PCs take ongoing damage as the encounter continues, though this damage depends on which Ring is chosen.

* *Water*: these actions always include the "evaluate options" action, which helps the party discern what actions they have available to them.  It's advisable for someone in the party to take this action early in an encounter.  Other Water actions let the PCs influence the scene directly.  A PCs who chooses the Path of Water automatically takes 5 serious wounds per round, in addition to any additional wounds taken by the action they choose.

* *Fire*: these actions are the most directly effective, but also the most dangerous.  They always include things which directly accomplish the goals of the scene, but you take the most damage.  People who choose the Path of Fire add an extra die to their pool of rolled dice, and automatically set that die to a 10, which is then rerolled as normal, but also takes 10 light wounds in addition to any wounds taken from the action they choose.

* *Earth*: these actions have their rolls divided in half, making them less effective than other rolls, and sometimes may only give bonuses to someone else's action rather than directly accomplishing anything.  However, these actions tend to cause less damage to the person who takes them, and people who choose the Path of Earth take no automatic light wounds.

* *Air*: these actions usually react to what others are doing, whether that means giving more information about the scene, reducing damage dealt to another PC, or boosting their roll like an Earth action would.  Players who choose the Path of Air take 5 light wounds if they take an action, however since Air is the final element to resolve its actions a player who chooses this path may choose to forgo their action and take no damage.

PCs choose their elemental path at the beginning of the round, but they are not required to choose the specific action within that path until their turn.  Thus, PCs acting in later paths may use information gained in the earlier paths to inform their decision of which action to take.

At the end of each round, players choose the path the take on the next round, with one restriction: they may choose any ring other than the one opposing their path on the previous round.  In other words, someone who chose the Path of Water may choose any path other than the Path of Fire for the next round.  The exception is that any PC may choose the *Path of Void* to go to an opposite ring (e.g. Air to Earth) - if they do this then the skills they roll will be chosen randomly.
