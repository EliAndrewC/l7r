"Between Places"
================

We have special rules when inhabiting "between places".  For example, people have sometimes gotten lost in the Shinomen Forest only a hundred miles from the Gateway to the Land of the Burning Sands and exited the Isawa Woodlands in Phoenix lands.  The Shifting Islands around the Mantis Isles are another example, where chidren playing have fallen asleep and woken up off the coast of Otosan Uchi.  Depending on the makeup of a cave you enter, you might find an exit out into the Ivory Kingdom.  Though of course, the most dangerous type of "between places" are the ones which are coexistent between different realms.  The Forgotten Tomb simultaneously exists in the mortal world and in Yomi, the realm of the honored dead.

Such places are strange and thus require different rules for establishing the success or failure of various actions.

Instead of making XkY rolls, you roll a dice pool and try to accumulate "successes".  A success occurs for every die in your pool which rolls a number equal to or higher than the adjusted difficulty for that roll.  For example, suppose someone is rolling 4 dice with an adjusted difficulty of 6 and they roll

> [2, 5, 6, 8]

That roll produces 2 successes.

Any die which rolls a 10 produces an automatic success regardless of difficulty, and then rerolls to see if it produces additional successes.  For example, suppose instead of the above someone rolled the following for their adjusted difficulty 6 roll:

> [2, 13, 17, 24]

This roll produced 5 successes.  Specifically, the roll initially produced three 10s.  One of those 10s rerolled to a 3, which is not an additional success.  One of them rerolled to a 7, which does produce an additional success.  One of them rerolled to another 10, which also produces an additional success and another reroll, but the reroll came to only a 4.

Physical actions combine void with one other ring to form the base dice pool.  Non-physical rolls use a single ring, e.g. assessing a situation has a base dice pool equal to your Water.

All rolls have a base difficulty and use two different skills.  (Athletics rolls substitute rings for one or both skills.)  Each rank in the skill someone has lowers the difficulty by 1.  Any free raises they recieve also decrease the difficulty by 1.  Anyone with a 3rd Dan technique which doesn't apply free raises to skills automatically has the difficulty of all rolls lowered by 1.  Anyone with a 5th Dan technique has the difficulty of all rolls lowered by 1.  Any bonus which is not expressed in free raises has its difficulty lowered by (bonus / 5) rounding down, and also gets an extra rolled die if there is any remainder.  Any extra rolled dice add directly to the die pool.

As an example, suppose Dairoku makes a precepts + underworld roll with a difficulty of 16 to assess the situation:

* Dairoku has 6 water, 3 precepts and 2 underworld.
* Dairoku's 5th Dan technique reduces the difficulty by 1.
* Dairoku has the Worldly advantage, which gives a free raise to underworld, which reduces the difficulty by 1.
* Dairoku's honor is 3.5, so he adds 7 to precepts rolls.  This reduces the difficulty by 1 and gives him an extra rolled die.

Putting that all together, Dairoku rolls 7 dice against an adjusted difficulty of 8.  Suppose Dairoku then rolls

> [1, 4, 5, 7, 8, 9, 17]

This is 3 successes, but Dairoku's 3rd Dan technique may be spent on underworld rolls, so if he wants, he can spend 1 of his free raises after seeing the roll to lower the difficulty by 1 to a new adjusted difficulty of 7 if he wants to get an extra 2 successes.

Each void point spent both increases the number of dice you roll by 1 and also lowers the difficulty by 1.
