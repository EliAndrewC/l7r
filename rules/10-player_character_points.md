# Player Character Points

PCs may take advantage of one rule which NPCs cannot; when things are really desperate, players can choose to spend a Player Character Point (PCP).  Here are the possible uses of PCPs:
- Once per roll, you may spend a PCP to give yourself a single free raise to any open, TN, or contested roll.
- You may spend a PCP to reroll any roll.  No roll may be rerolled more than once, even if a previous reroll was made through a different ability.
- When making a roll while impaired, after seeing the result of a roll for which 10s can ever be rerolled (e.g. not initiative and not the strike portion of an iaijutsu duel), you may spend a PCP to ignore your impaired status and reroll the 10s.

Spending PCPs costs Experience Points (XP).  The first time a character spends a PCP, they lose 1 XP.  If a PCP is spent without an XP available, the character loses the next 1 XP they would have earned.  The second time a charactr spends a PCP, the cost is 2 XP, the third time the cost is 3 XP, etc.
