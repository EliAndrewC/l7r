This is the first time we've ever take a shot at mass combat rules in L7R, so this is somewhat experimental.

Combat is divided into *stages* - basically the same as combat rounds, except that a stage represents about 10 minutes of combat, so the rolls made represent an abstraction over a great many different actions and interactions.  Each stage may contain 1, 2 or 3 *phases*, depending on the intensity of the fighting in your area.


h2. Fighting posture

Each PCs individually determines how much they are focusing on dealing damage to the other side vs keeping themselves (and those around them) from being overwhelmed by opposing forces.

At the beginning of each phase of combat, each PC individually chooses one of the following fighting postures:

* *Defensive fighting:*  The PC will focus on holding the battle line together, protecting themselves and their comrades, and damaging enemies who try to engage with them.  This is a good posture to take when already heavily wounded (especially if you expect to be able to fall back and heal soon) or when your side has such an advantage that you expect the other side to inevitably be ground down and defeated.

* *Offensive fighting:*  The PC will focus on dealing the most damage possible to the opposing side, taking more damage themselves in the process.  Taking this posture maximizes the chances of heroic opportunity and gains of recognition, but it's unlikely that even the most seasoned warrior will be able to take this posture in every encounter.

* *Reactive fighting:*  Basically the middle ground of the above options, the PC looks for opportunities to press an attack, but isn't committed to making any heavty charges or heavy engagements.

See below for how these postures affect the amount of damage you both deal and take.


h2. Level of engagement

Each PC rolls for their own individual fighting, but this is heavily affected by the location of their unit within the theater of operations.  The main thing affecting these rolls is how heavily engaged you are with enemy forces.

At a high-level, your unit will be one of the following:

* *disengaged* - not actively fighting, but rather waiting in reserve waiting until other units need to fall back, waiting to take their place.  PCs can heal light wounds while disengaged.

* *skirmishing* - engaged with the enemy, but mainly through ranged combat, feints, and hit-and-run tactics, in which neither side is attempting a full charge against the other.

* *clashing* - engaged with the enemy in close quarters with heavy fighting.

* *intense fighting* - this is usually avoided, but sometimes military units find opportunity to secure an advantage by pressing forward, inflicting heavy casualties on an enemy in exchange for engaging in the heaviest fighting on the battlefield.


h3. Disengaged

Many battles consist of both sides having their units repeatedly cycle between skirmishing with the opposing force and falling back into reserve to bandage their wounds and regroup for the next charge.  In open battles, this can last for hours as each side tries to wear the other down while assessing where the enemy would be especially weak to a charge.

Remember that light wounds don't automatically heal after a fight, but treatment and rest can make them go away completely.  Unfortunately, in the midst of battle there often isn't the luxury of applying the sorts of cleaning, bandages, medicines, and prayers which can accomplish this.  However, being in the reserves is likely to decrease light wounds taken, as one can often dismount and apply some rudimentary first aid.

Each stage spent disengaged allows a PC to *heal (2 x Earth)kX light wounds*, where X is the number of consecutive stages spent disengaged.

Each stage spent in reserves, there is a chance that this healing will not happen due to enemy forces forcing your unit to re-engage before being able to regroup and administer first aid.  There is also a separate chance of this happening at the end of each stage, which means that even if a unit intends to spend multiple stages resting and healing, they may not be afforded the opportunity.  The chances of this depend greatly on the tide of battle, and are usually going to be somewhere in the neighborhood of 10-25% and 20-50%, respectively.  The better a battle is going for your side, the less chance there is of being forced into this kind of re-engagement, though even in an overwhelmingly successful battle a unit may still be called upon to help stabilize a position or participate in a coordinated strike.  The chances also go up over time, as other units have been fighting for longer will be more likely to need to be reinforced.

A unit may advance from disengaged to either skirmishing or clashing, though there is a chance that advancing to clashing will fail if the other side feints/moves to make it only a skirmish.


h3. Skirmishing

Each stage you are engaged in a skirmish, the GM makes a *10kX damage roll* against you, where X is 2, 3, or 4 based on whether you are fighting defensively, responsively, or offensively.  You make a wound check as normal, with the following additional modifications:
* subtract your strategy skill from the light wounds taken
* 5th Dan characters subtract 5 from the light wounds taken
* bushi schools subtract 5 from their light wounds taken, and parry schools also get a free raise on their wound check

Each PC also makes a 10kY damage roll representing how much harm they have inflicted on the other side, where Y is 2, 5, or 10 based on their fighting posture.  This damage roll gets the following bonuses:
* add your strategy skill
* 5th Dan characters get a free raise
* bushi schools get 2 free raises, or one free raise if they are a parry school
* you get a free raise for each point above 3 your Fire and Void rings are (and -10 for each point below 3 they each are)
* all abilities which add directly to the result of either attack or damage rolls add directly to this roll; abilities which add to both attack and damage get added twice
* any extra rolled dice on attack or damage add an additional +2 to this roll and any extra kept dice add +5

The damage you deal affect three things:
# How well your side is doing as a whole, which will be randomly determined by the GM with your actions affecting the likelihood of a positive result.
# The amount of personal glory you gain, which will translate into Recognition bonuses after the battle.
# Your likelihood for heroic opportunities (see below).

A unit which is skirmishing may fall back into the reserves and enter the disengaged state at the end of a stage of battle.  There is a chance this will fail, as the other side may not make it easy/possible to disengage, even with a unit attempting to reinforce you.

A skirmishing unit may also attempt a charge, forcing the other side into a clashing state.  There is a chance this will fail, as well as a chance that it succeeds in a way that puts your unit into intense fighting.

The chances of transition are largely determined by how well the battle is going overall for your side.  Most withdrawls into the reserves usually work, and most charges from a position of skirmish do succeed in clashing with the enemy.


h3. Clashing

Clashing gives you a higher chance of heroic opportunities relative to skirmishing, especially ones which involve being able to find and exploit weaknesses in the enemy's formations.

Clashing uses the same rules as skirmishing, but you make and receive two damage rolls during the stage of combat.  Between making these two rolls, each PC may adjust their fighting posture, and the GM will also make adjustments to the tide of battle.

A unit which is clashing may attempt to fall back to either a skirmishing or a disengaged state, with a percentage chance of either failing.  A failure to fall back into the reserves may result in either a complete failure to disengage which results in the unit continuing to clash in the next stage of combat, or a partial success in which they fall back into a skirmishing state.


h3. Intense Fighting

This usually represents going behind enemy lines or being equivalently surrounded or outnumbered.  It is therefore generally intended to be a *cost* associated with a unit taking a heroic opportunity.  Damage is dealt/received as above, but with three rolls being made instead of one or two.  As with clashing, PCs may adjust their fighting posture between rolls, and the GM will adjust the tide of battle.

A unit in intense fighting can at most withdraw back to a skirmishing position, and will likely be forced to withdraw into a clashing engagement.


h3. Engagement transitions

As mentioned above, you can't transition more than 2 levels of engagement.  Even transitioning one level may fail, e.g.

* A skirmishing unit that attempts a charge to engage in a clash with the opposing unit may find their charge fails due to the enemy successfully dividing and regrouping to avoid engaging in more than a skirmish.

* A clashing unit which tries to disengage may fail and only fall back to a skirmish instead rather than successfully withdrawing into the reserves.

Here is a table of the chances that a unit attempting to change from one level of engagement to another.  As a general rule, it's easier to engage more heavily than to withdraw, and enemy units usually prefer skirmishing to clashing and thus will resist being pulled into a full clash.  These numbers are all affected by the tide of battle.

| Chance of
succeeding: | *Disengaged* | *Skirmish* | *Clash* | *Intense* |
| *Disengaged* | - | 80% | 20% | - |
| *Skirmish* | 50% | - | 50% | 20% |
| *Clash* | 20% | 50% | - | 80% |
| *Intense* | - | 20% | 80% | - |

Note that the easiest withdrawal is downgrading from intense combat into "only" clashing, because this usually involves charging towards a group of enemies!

There's also a chance at each level that if you try to remain at that level you may be pulled into another level.  A unit you're skirmishing with may withdraw entirely, or they may attempt a charge to clash with you, etc.

Here are those chances, which are also affected by the tide of battle.  These numbers only come up if you attempt to remain at the same level of engagement:

| Chance of being
forced into: | *Disengaged* | *Skirmish* | *Clash* | *Intense* |
| *Disengaged* | - | 20% | 10% | - |
| *Skirmish* | 10% | - | 20% | 1% |
| *Clash* | - | 20% | - | 10%|
| *Intense* | - | 1% | 20% | -|

Note that if you're pulled into combat from a disengaged state, there's a 50% chance this happens before you were able to heal.


h2. Tide of battle

The GM determines the tide of battle when combat begins and then re-computes it each stage.

The tide of battle for your unit's engagement represents how well your unit is doing against the enemy unit you are currently engaged with.  This is affected by both the overall tide of battle and your unit's performance.  The higher your damage rolls, the more this will be affected.

The tide of battle does not affect the damage rolls or wound checks in a battle directly.  Instead, it affects how likely your unit is to be able to transition between levels of engagement, and how likely you are to be presented with different types of heroic opportunities.

Characters can make *strategy* rolls to assess the tide of battle when making decisions - for the leader this roll automatically happens as a contested strategy roll against the enemy commanding officer.

The local tide of battle may be evenly matched all the way up to one side or another being routed.  The PCs are affected only by the local tide of battle.  The GM keeps track of the global tide of battle, which both affects and is affected by the PCs local engagement with their directly-opposing enemies.

The local tide of battle has the following possible levels, each of which affect the chance of personal heroic opportunities, unit heroic opportunities, and successfully maneuvering between levels of engagement.  Each tide of battle level is worth 5%, as indicated by the following table listing the status of your enemy's battle lines and the resulting adjustment:

| *Level* | *Adjustment* |
| PC forces routed | -30% |
| PC forces scattering | -25% |
| PC lines breaking | -20% |
| PC lines buckling | -15% |
| PC lines wavering | -10% |
| PC lines holding | -5% |
| both sides matched | 0% |
| enemy lines holding | 5% |
| enemy lines wavering | 10% |
| enemy lines buckling | 15% |
| enemy lines breaking | 20% |
| enemy forces scattering | 25% |
| enemy forces routed | 30% |

If the local tide of battle reaches the "routed" level, then it will likely be reset to something lower as the rest of their side has to reinforce it.  However, this will have a direct, immediate, and visible effect on the global tide of battle.  This will bring great glory and shame to the side doing the routing and being routed, respectively.

In addition to the current level of the tide of battle, each side will have additional tide of battle adjustments in increments smaller than 5%:

-> Pre-battle speeches will always have an impact.  These consist of three rolls: bragging, open sincerity, and culture (history free raises apply to this culture roll).  There's a baseline standard derived from a comparison to the opponents.  Each 5 above or below that threshold is worth a plus or minus 1% change.  The threshold may be different for each roll, and depending on the circumstances there may be bonuses or penalties depending on whether each roll is made by the same person or if different people make each roll.

-> At the beginning of the battle, opposing leaders make contested strategy rolls, with a 1% change for each increment of 5 difference between them.  This roll is made again during a change in leadership, such as when a battalion commander is killed - this new roll will typically come with a penalty to the new commanding officer.

-> Opposing leaders make an additional contested strategy roll between stage of combat.  This roll also evaluates the state of the battlefield.  The winner gets a flat 1% bonus to their tide of battle percentage.

Heroic opportunities can shift the tide of battle directly.  Additionally, between each phase of each stage, there's a chance of the tide shifting in the PCs' favor.  Each damage roll is compared to a circumstantial TN, and every increment of 5 above that TN results in a 1% chance of a shift.  This TN is 50 minus the PCs' current tide of battle adjustment.

There is also a random chance of the battle shifting against the PCs, which is a flat percentage chance of X% (determined by the GM based on the circumstances of the battle, but usually in the range of 10-20%) minus half the PCs' current tide of battle adjustment, to a minimum of 5%.


h2. Heroic Opportunities

The higher someone rolls on damage, the more of a chance they have of getting a Heroic Opportunity.  After the end of a stage, there are unit-level heroic opportunities which usually involve the entire unit collectively pursuing some objective.  The chances of generating heroic opportunities is primarily driven by how much damage the PCs deal.


h3. Individual heroic opportunities

The GM rolls for heroic opportunities for each PC after their damage rolls are recorded but before their damage and wound checks are resolved.  The individual heroic opportunities occur during the stage in which those rolls were made.

Here are the baseline chances of a heroic opportunity at each level (these numbers are affected by the tide of battle, see below):
* Heroic opportunities do not occur while disengaged.
* While skirmishing there's a baseline chance of 0% of a heroic opportunity.  If you roll at least 60 damage, the chances are increased to 1%, plus 1% per increment of 5 above 60 damage you roll.
* While clashing, the baseline chance is 5%, plus another 5% if you roll at least 50 damage, plus 1% for every increment of 5 above 50 damage you roll.
* While engaged in intense fighting, the baseline chance is 10%, plus X% where X is the amount your damage roll exceeds 40.

Here's a basic list of heroic opportunities:


*A Good Day to Die*
The enemy lines immediately opposite you are breaking, and you can roll up their entire unit by plunging into the breach.  This will find you surrounded by frantic enemies desperately trying to repel you before they are pushed back.  Take 1 automatic serious wound to raise the tide of battle 1 full level in your side's favor.

*A Clear Shot*
The front lines break, and you have a clear shot at the leader of the opposing unit with a ranged attack!  This shot is unlikely to slay the enemy leader, but may impact the tide of battle.  Make an attack roll.  If you roll at least a 25, the leader is winged and is seen as having to wheel around and retreat to safety despite not being hurt - shift the tide of battle adjustment 1% in your favor.  If you roll at least a 40, you've made a direct hit and he is additionally seen to be wounded - add another 1% to your tide of battle adjustment.  If you roll at least a 60, he is forced to transfer command to a subordinate in order to have the arrow removed, triggering a change-of-command contested strategy roll at a penalty for the other side.

*A Samurai Never Stands Alone*
You find yourself in absolute coordination with the rest of your unit, such that your blows are in tune with those of your comrades, with you driving the flow of the fighting.  Make an attack roll and add the result to your unit's chances of a heroic opportunity this stage.

*Be Prepared to Dig Two Graves*
A member of the enemy’s army cuts down one of your kinsmen.  You can focus on him, but only by taking an Offensive Fighting posture on the next roll.  After you make your next damage roll, also make an attack roll - if the combination of attack and damage add up to 100 then you have slain them.  If not then you may or may not be able to repeat this based on whether their unit withdraws.

*Break Morale*
You just led your squad through another squad of enemies, quickly killing two of them and scattering the rest.  Make an intimidation roll with a stepped TN of 20 / 40 / 60 to shake the other side.  On a 20, the rest of their squad retreats, on a 40 your unit's transition will automatically succeed, and on a 60 their whole unit withdraws to be reinforced at the end of the current stage.

*Breaking Blow*
Having already dealt a devastating attack, you can give your allies cover by pressing your advantage.  Choose an Offensive Fighting posture next phase to heal everyone else in your unit by (2 x Earth)k2 light wounds this phase (resolve this after damage is dealt but before wound checks are made).

*Clear a Path*
[Only on the last phase of a stage.]  You have a chance to push the enemy back in order to ensure the success of your unit's next maneuver.  If you do, take 50 damage and make a parry roll to reduce the damage taken by the result of your parry roll in addition to the normal reductions.  Your unit will automatically succeed at its maneuver this stage if you attempt to change your level of engagement, or you will automatically stay at your current level otherwise.

*Chasing the Wind*
Sometimes you find yourself in the perfect spot for the moment, and other times you realize that the opportune place is difficult to reach and that you must move quickly to get there.  You have spotted the ideal place to deploy those under your command, but it will require weaving through battle and avoiding charging enemies to succeed.  Make an Air roll (twice Air keep Air) against a TN of 30 to move to where you need to be.  If you fail the TN, then take an extra 10 light wounds this phase.  Add 1% to your side's tide of battle adjustment if you fail, and 2% if you succeed.

*Counterattack*
Your enemy has overextended and left themselves vulnerable.  Take an Offensive Fighting posture next phase to add 1% to your tide of battle adjustment.

*Create Distraction*
[Only on the last phase of a stage.] You are perfectly positioned to draw the enemy's attention, guaranteeing the success of your side's next move.  If you take a Defensive Fighting posture next phase, your unit will automatically succeed at its maneuver this stage if you attempt to change your level of engagement, or you will automatically stay at your current level guaranteed to not being forced into a different level.

*Crushing Attack*
You've isolated an opponent, who is scrambling to rejoin his comrades.  Make a 10k2 damage roll with the usual bonuses against a TN of 40 minus the current tide of battle adjustment to slay him before he gets the chance.

*Delicate Calculations*
[Only on the last phase of a stage.] As your unit prepares to reorient its forces, you find yourself well-positioned to redeploy the troops under your command to set your entire unit up for future success.  Make a strategy roll and divide the result by 5.  Add the result to the chances of a heroic opportunity for every phase of the following stage.

*Diversionary Tactics*
By repeatedly appearing about to charge, you are able to hold back your enemies, giving your comrades time to regroup and tend to their wounds without withdrawing to the reserves.  Adopt a Defensive Fighting posture next phase to let all other members of your unit heal by (2 x Earth)k2 light wounds (resolve this after damage is dealt but before wound checks are made).

*Drawing Fire*
You and your comrades are between the enemy and the rest of your unit, and well-positioned to shield them to allow for better regrouping.  You may take 50 damage and make a parry roll to reduce the damage taken by the result of your parry roll in addition to the normal reductions.  If you do, everyone else in your unit may heal by (2 x Earth)k2 light wounds after they take damage and before they make their wound checks this phase.

*Few Against Many*
Half a unit is surrounded by enemies.  Your unit rushes to strike against those surrounding them.  You can hope that your counterattack will be sufficient to rescue your comrades, or push your way through, taking an extra 10kX damage (X=2/3/4 at 60% / 30% / 10%) to reinforce them enough that they will no longer be surrounded.  If you push through, then make a parry roll with a TN of 50 minus the tide of battle adjustment - success means that no one is killed, and failure means one member of the friendly unit is killed plus another for every 10 you fail by.

*Greater Destiny*
Your ancestors are with you, and you can feel them pulling the connections of fate and circumstance.  Reroll twice for 2 different heroic opportunities.

*Let Them Escape*
As the opposing force falls back, most units would pursue, but a quick look at your surroundings makes you realize that their retreat has given your side a much more valuable opportunity to secure the ground you've already gained and solidify your forces.  Make a strategy roll - everyone in your unit heals half that many light wounds (resolve this after damage for this phase is dealt but before wound checks are made).

*Martyr*
A member of your unit has fallen in battle.  This is an opportunity to remind your forces how fortunate your fallen comrade was, having died a glorious death, and how it now falls to the rest of his unit to avenge him.  Subtract 2% from your current tide of battle adjustment, then make a bragging roll and divide the result by 20 (rounding down) and add the result to your current tide of battle adjustment.  Your bragging roll determines whether your soldiers see this as a glorious opportunity or a horrible loss.

*Open the Way*
At the nexus of a pivotal point in the battle, you are able to personally generate a Unit-level heroic opportunity, which the commanding officer of your unit must still choose whether to accept.  All eyes are on you in this moment, and you will receive full credit as the person who created the opening for your unit.

*Pick up the Banner*
[May not happen on the last phase of a stage.]  You see the banner carrier fall to arrows.  It will eventually get picked up, but you can ensure this happens sooner by taking a Reactive or higher fighting posture in the next phase of the current stage.

*Rallying Cry*
Your side has been scattered by the chaos of battle and needs to be regrouped.  Make a bragging roll to gather everyone together back to your banner - the TN is 60 minus the current tide of battle adjustment - if you miss the TN then take a 1% penalty to your tide of battle adjustment.

*Refuse Advantage*
An enemy warrior has fallen off his horse and is hopelessly separated from his platoon.  Despite being heavily wounded, he is refusing to surrender and is instead shouting defiantly as you and your soldiers close the gap.  In a moment he will be overwhelmed, but his comrades will see him die a warrior's death and will suffer no penalty for having watched their friend die.  You may choose to motion for your men to hang back so that you can finish him yourself - if you do then make a 10k2 damage roll with the usual bonuses and take 50 light wounds minus the result to slay him.  Add 1% to your tide of battle adjustment for showing the enemy that even their fiercest warrior can still be slain by a single samurai - by you.

*Save a Wounded Comrade*
In the midst of battle, you notice a kinsman has fallen off their horse and is about to be swarmed by enemies!  You have the opportunity to save him - take an extra 10kX damage (X=2/3/4 at 60% / 30% / 10%) to be able to make a parry roll to ensure he isn't killed - the TN is 30 minus the current tide of battle adjustment.

*Shallow Victory*
[Only when the PC's battle adjustment is negative.] Both your forces and your enemy's forces need to be made to understand that their current advantage is fleeting.  Make a bragging roll with a TN 50 minus the current tide of battle adjustment.  Move the tide of battle 1% in your favor if you succeed, plus another 1% for each increment of 5 you exceed the TN.

*Stand Against the Waves*
[Only when the PC's tide of battle adjustment is negative.] The enemy is trying to press their advantage by sending all available forces forward, and you are in a position to thwart them.  Take an extra 10kX damage (X=2/3/4 at 60% / 30% / 10%) to make a 10k2 damage roll with the usual bonuses.  Add X% to your side's tide of battle adjustment, where X is your damage roll divided by 10.

*Strike Without Striking*
The enemy is trying to reposition themselves, trying to reunite several units prior to re-engaging.  You recognize that the best way to keep them off-balance is to neither attack nor retreat, but to position your soldiers to prevent the enemy forces from being able to coalesce.  Make a strategy roll to add X% to your side's tide of battle adjustment, where X is the strategy roll divided by 20.

*The Path Not Taken*
You find yourself free to move like the wind, redeploying your troops to block and confound the enemy.  Make an Air roll (twice Air keep Air) to cause everyone in your unit to heal light wounds equal to your roll divided by 5 (resolved after damage is dealt this phase but before wound checks are made).

*The Price of Failure*
Your enemies are trying to push you back, but you can taunt and frustrate them by not giving in.  If you choose to keep your light wounds this phase, then add the damage dealt to you (before reductions) to the light wounds you were already taking and divide by 10.  Add the result to the tide of battle adjustment, having dashed your enemy's hopes and impaired their morale.

*Tricked*
[Only on the last phase of a stage.] You've been carefully watching you enemy watching you, and are able to mislead them about your unit's next move by preparing your men for an intentionally wrong signal - a signal which your enemy believes themselves to have learned.  Make an Air roll (twice Air keep Air) and increase your unit's chance of a successful maneuver into a different level of engagement by the result.  If you choose not to change your level of engagement, your chance of being pulled into a different level of engagement is eliminated for the current stage and the next stage.

*Unfettered Attack*
You find that your push forward is more successful than expected as the enemy was unprepared for the onslaught.  Make a 10k2 damage roll with the usual bonuses and increase the tide of battle adjustment 1% times the result of the roll divided by 20 (rounding down).

*When Men Stand Divided*
The opposing forces were instantly scattered by your attack, rendering them completely unable to oppose you effectively as they were driven back to frantically rejoin the ranks of their allies.  Take no damage this phase.

*Your Life is Mine*
The savagery of your assault has unsettled the enemy, prompting them to focus more on survival than harming your side.  You find yourself able to strengthen their fear - make an intimidation roll and divide the result by 5 to heal that many light wounds for everyone in your unit (resolve this after damage is dealt but before wound checks have been made this phase).


h3. Unit-level heroic opportunities

This is something that the PCs (or their designated unit commander) must decide at the end of a battle stage if given the opportunity.  Unit-level heroic opportunities are revealed before players make their wound checks for the last phase of a stage, but do not trigger until after the wound checks have been resolved.  Players can therefore make decisions about those wound checks with foreknowledge of an upcoming unit-level heroic opportunity.  The final decision on whether to take the heroic opportunity is made after the wound checks have fully resolved, so unit leaders may make that decision based on the final outcome of wound checks.

Each phase, the damage roll of each PC is divided by 5 (rounded down), and the results are added together.  That is the percentage chance of generating a unit-level heroic opportunity at the end of the stage.  Multiple unit-level heroic opportunities may be generated, but unless otherwise specified only 1 may be taken, and the unit commander must chose which opportunity (if any) is taken.  Also remember that the tide of battle raises or lowers this percentage.

bq. *NOTE:* any time this section references "each PC", this assumes that each PC is commanding a sub-unit within the main unit maneuvering between different levels of engagement within the battle.  For example, in most battles the PCs would be platoon lieutenants within the same company, though in a larger battle they could be company captains within a battalion, etc.  If there are fewer than 4 PC sub-unit officers, and NPC officer would add their roll as well.  This is relevant for the Tuesday group, where [[:mirumoto-no-ryusei-akane]] is a platoon lieutenant in the main company of the Western Star main battalion, and whose rolls are therefore used as if she was a PC.


*Another Time*
Enemy forces have coalesced a distance from you.  While you may re-engage with them, as they are clearly expecting, they have given you the opportunity to withdraw at an unhurried pace.  You may automatically fall back one level of engagement in order to automatically let everyone heal (2 x Earth)k2 light wounds.

*Attack their Reserves*
Your unit has a path that leads directly to the disengaged troops of the opposing army.  You may automatically move one level of engagement deeper to get to them.  They are currently scattered and not well-formed, so your attack will be devastating and automatically move the tide of battle 1 level in your favor.

*Break the Line*
The enemy's lines are faltering and seem about to break.  You can automatically charge one level more engaged to scatter their lines - this maneuver will automatically succeed, and every maneuver after that has a (100 - 20X)% success bonus, where X is the number of stages of combat after this successful charge.

*Catching the Wind's Favor*
You've found yourselves blessed by the Air kami.  Your next maneuver will automatically succeed, and you will not be forced into another level of engagement until you chose to either advance or withdraw.

*Chase them down*
The enemy unit suffered such heavy losses that they were forced into a hasty retreat.  If you push one level deeper into engagement, you can turn their retreat into a route, scattering the opposing forces.  Your maneuver for this phase will automatically succeed, and if you do choose to push forward, your next maneuver will automatically succeed as well, and the chances of heroic opportunities is increased by 20% for the entire next stage.

*Coordinated Strike*
The PCs' unit has been given a golden opportunity to push the enemy back from a valuable part of the battlefield.  This will require all members of the unit acting as one, with little opportunity for individual heroics.  If this opportunity is taken, move the tide of battle 1 level in your favor, but all PCs must select Reactive Fighting for the first phase of the next stage, and everyone's chances of a personal heroic opportunity for that phase is automatically 0%.  This opportunity is unaffected by any change to the unit's level of engagement.

*Cornered*
An unfortunate confluence of circumstances has left your unit cornered between multiple enemy units.  They are attempting to push you back in order to force a retreat.  You may have everyone take an extra 10kX damage (X=2/3/4 at 60% / 30% / 10%).  If you do not, the tide of battle shifts 1 level against you and you are automatically pushed back one level of engagement.

*Deploy Reserves*
The reserves of neighboring units are being cycled forward.  If you do not order your own reserves forward as well, your area of the battlefield will become uncoordinated.  You may automatically fall back into the reserves, and if you do not then the tide of battle shifts 1 level against you.

*Enemy Hesitation*
Your ferocity has caused opposing forces to hesitate, reluctant to press any attack.  This has given you a chance to tend to your wounded without lifting any of the pressure you yourselves are able to place on your enemies.  Everyone is able to automatically heal (2 x Earth)k2 light wounds.

*Entrench*
Your unit has found itself in a favorable position.  You may automatically maintain your current level of engagement for another stage in order to move the tide of battle 1 level in your favor.

*Fake Retreat*
The enemy seems to active as if they have gained an advantage, giving you the opportunity to exploit their foolish underestimation of your strength.  You make have everyone in your unit take 10kX damage (X=2/3/4 at 60% / 30% / 10%) and fall back one level of engagement to shift the tide of battle 1 level in your favor.

*Feint*
You sense a weakness in the enemy's lines that you are unfortunately not positioned to effectively exploit.  However, you may be able to cause them to overreact, forcing the enemy leader into an unfavorable troop deployment pattern.  Force all members of your unit into a Defensive fighting position at the beginning of the next stage of combat to move the tide of battle 1 level in your favor.

*Not This Day*
The enemy has tried to isolate your unit to cut off any possible retreat, which would force other units to overextend into a disadvantageous position to prevent disaster.  You may attempt to retreat back into a less-engaged state.  If you do not attempt this or do not succeed, then shift the tide of battle 1 level against you.

*Outflank*
Your enemy has allowed you to outmaneuver them, forcing them back.  Automatically move the tide of battle 1 level in your favor.

*Overwhelm*
You and the troops in your unit see an enemy commander in the midst of battle with a severely light personal guard.  If you move one level of engagement higher in the next stage, you will be able to engage directly with the enemy commander and likely kill him and his personal guard.

*Pressure*
Success begets success, and your accomplishments in this battle have opened the way for further accomplishments.  All PCs have an extra 30% chance of a personal Heroic Opportunity in the first phase of the next stage.

*Provide Cover*
A neighboring unit is staging a tactical withdrawal into the reserves but is finding it difficult to disengage.  Your unit is able to deploy to provide them the cover they need to withdraw.  Everyone in your unit takes 40 damage and makes a parry roll, reducing the damage taken by the result of the parry roll, to a minimum of zero.

*Punch Through*
Your devastating attack has pushed the enemy back, opening a way for your forces to move.  All PCs make an extra 10k2 damage roll (without taking damage themselves).  Sum the results - this is the percentage bonus to your next maneuver to change your level of engagement, and you will not be forced into a different level until that time.  If this bonus pushes your chance of success above 100%, the excess is applied to the next roll, and so forth.

*Regroup*
The flow of battle has naturally given you a brief reprieve from the fighting to rally your forces and tend to your wounds.  As long as you either maintain your current level of engagement or fall back to a lower level of engagement, everyone in your unit heals (2 x Earth)k2 light wounds.

*Ride to the Rescue*
A neighboring unit is in trouble and you can move in to save the day!  You may automatically set your level of engagement to clashing for the next stage to provide them the opportunity to make a tactical retreat in order to reassemble their scattered forces.

*Shout With One Voice*
Banzai!  Your attack has caused the enemy to falter, and a cry of ferocity is going up from your unit to dismay them.  All PCs should make intimidation rolls, which are summed together and divided by 20.  Move the tide of battle adjustment that percent in your favor.

*Stand Together*
Your unit has managed to exert control over the area of the battlefield they currently hold, which they can maintain so long as they hold their lines tightly.  Force all PCs to adopt a Defensive Fighting posture in the first phase of the next stage in order to guarantee success the next time you attempt to maneuver into a different level of engagement, and prevent you from being forced into another level until then.

*Stand Your Ground*
You must maintain your current level of engagement for another stage.  If you choose not to do so, your unit will be fine, but the tide of battle will shift 1 level against you.

*Take Prisoners*
Your unit has managed to isolate a squad of enemy warriors.  Several horses fell over with several riders being grounded.  If you stay in your current orientation for one more stage, you'll be able to take them into custody, and then you can attempt a withdrawal back to the reserves with prisoners at a lower-than-usual chance of success, given that the other side may press to rescue their captured allies.

*Take the Enemy Banner*
The enemy's banner is almost within reach - you need only maintain your current level of engagement for another stage.

*Test of Courage*
(Reroll for a different opportunity if the tide of battle is already in your unit's favor.)  Although the battle is going against you, you can show the enemy that your resolve is unbroken.  Maintain your current level of engagement to move the tide of battle 2 levels in your favor.

*The Turtle's Shell*
Your unit has found a favorable position from which the enemy is not able to easily attack you.  Force everyone in your unit to adopt a defensive fighting posture for the first stage of the next phase in order to allow everyone to heal (2 x Earth)k2 light wounds.

*Those Who Stand Alone*
Your unit has become temporarily isolated from the rest of your forces.  You can turtle your forces to keep everyone safe, or take the opportunity for glory by having everyone in your unit take 10kX damage (X=2/3/4 at 60% / 30% / 10%).  If you do this, the opportunity for personal heroic opportunities for the all phases of the next stage is increased by the result of the highest damage roll.

*Time of Destiny*
Your unit is at the right place at the right time for the thick of battle for many glorious events to occur in succession.  Reroll twice and get two different opportunities.  (If the second opportunity is mutually exclusive with the first one, reroll the second one until you end up with two valid options.)

*Unexpected Allies*
A neighboring unit has managed to maneuver into position for a devastating pincer attack.  Each PC gets to make an extra 10k2 damage roll (taking no extra wounds themselves).  Add the total damage together and divide by 25 - that is the percentage that the tide of battle shifts in your favor.  (To clarify, this is direct percentage points rather than tide of battle levels.)

*Wedge*
The enemy has left a weak spot in their lines, leaving themselves vulnerable to a "flight of cranes" wedge attack.  Move the tide of battle 1 level in your favor, but at the cost of everyone in your unit taking 10kX damage (X=2/3/4 at 60% / 30% / 10%).

*Your Last Mistake*
The enemy thinks you weak in a place where you may think yourself strong.  They are mustering for a charge, and you must decide whether or not to meet them head on.  Force everyone in your unit into an Offensive fighting posture at the beginning of the next stage in order to move the tide of battle 1 level in your favor.
